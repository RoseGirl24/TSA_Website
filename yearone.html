Principles of Engineering


Course Summary: Principles of Engineering is a first year, introductory STEM course. This class provides an overview of various disciplines within the fields of engineering and technology. The concepts covered include the engineering design process, orthographic and isometric drawing, dimensioning, the basics of CAD, and design communication. Students use the engineering design process to produce simple products, both individually and in groups.

This class is taught by Mrs. Murphy and is typically taken by students in grades nine or ten.


Typical Assignments

Instant Challenges

Instant Challenges are assigned only for one or two class periods. They are smaller assignments that require quick innovation and creation of a simple product. These often include limited materials or other restraints to incease difficulty and thus invention. Some examples include creating a simple device from household objects and pitching it to the class, or trying to build a strong tower out of materials such as paper and pipe cleaners.

Principles of Engineering often assigns Instant Challenges. They are an engaging way to develop and demonstrate skills, and are a useful tool to test students' understanding of concepts.

The puzzle cube is an in-depth individual project that involves design and isometric drawings, the use of CAD to create three dimensional models and multi-view drawings, the construction and testing of a prototype, and reflection on possible modifications.

Projects

Projects are longer assignments, lasting up to a few weeks. Rather than have tests, this class tends to give these assignments an equivalent importance in grading. They involve bringing together skills and concepts learned to create products such as puzzle cubes, paper roller coasters, or wooden catapults.

Projects also provide an opportunity for students to practice effective communication, either by working in teams or by presenting their individual work. The projects assigned in Principles of Engineering attempt to simulate, at a high school level, the basics of what a college or career assignment may be like in the engineering and technology fields.

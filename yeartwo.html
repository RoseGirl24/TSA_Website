Engineering Design I


Course Summary: Engineering Design I is a second year STEM course designed for students interested in developing skills for post-secondary careers in the fields of engineering and technology. Students examine potential careers and educational institutions. They learn about concepts such as simple and complex machines, Ohm's law, energy and energy calculations, and material analysis and selection. They also continue to work on projects, both individually and in small groups, and develop communication and presentation skills.

This class is taught by Mr. Guthrie and is typically taken by students in grades ten or eleven.


Learning By Doing

Even more so than the first year class, Engineering Design I puts a focus on learning through projects, both individual and team-based. In fact, many assignments are largely student directed. Although help is available if it is requested, in many cases students are given an objective and allowed to find their own solution within a set of restrictions.

Why Student Led?

Although this course still includes the traditional lecture and occasional worksheet, a large majority of the class is spent on the projects described above. This has a number of benefits:

  • Increases creativity and innovation
  • Encourages students to solve problems
  • Better prepares students for higher education and the workforce
  • Helps to improve time management and communication skills

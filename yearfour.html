Engineering Practicum


Course Summary: Engineering Practicum is a fourth year capstone course. The main focus of this course is the development of an engineering- or technology-based senior thesis. A professional enviornment allows students to refine and apply the skills learned in the program in perperation for university or a career.

This class is taught by Mr. Guthrie and is taken by students in grade twelve.


What Is The Senior Thesis?

Central Magnet seniors dedicate a large amount of their last year of high school to the development of a senior thesis. This extensive project can take the form of in-depth research, analyzing and building upon existing evidence, or the development of a product. Students on the engineering pathway use the engineering design process to design, construct, and refine a product before presenting it at an end-of-year expo. This is a chance for students to demonstrate their knowledge of STEM concepts and add a large product to their portfolio for college or job applications.
